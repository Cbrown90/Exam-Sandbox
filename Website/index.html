SUMMARY OF MARK UP TAGS

Semantic mark up is used for:

  • Search Engines
  • Non visual desktop access (Daredevil's PC)
  • Other Web Crealers

It is invisible on the page and divs should be used for css.

Brief Summary of all tags:

<header>
Area at top of element/page, more word header and footer than Heading.
<footer>
Area at bottom of element/page, opposite of header. Header not needed for footer.
<nav>
Major series of links. Not ite map in footer.
<aside>
Content not directly related to content of element the Aside is in. Example advert in main page or side note in article.
<section>
Part of page/article with its own header.
<article>
Content that can stand on it's own and be moved to other locations and still make sense. e.g googles front page summarys picks these up.

USING ROLES

Roles are used for to provide meaning to screen readers for accessibility purposes.

Example of roles:

main
The main part of a page. Max 1 per page
form
Area containing input forms
navigation
Area with navigational links

More at: https://www.w3.org/TR/wai-aria/roles#role_definitions

Annotating content

These tags will have both an imapact on the page and have meaning for search engines.

Example of elements annotating text:

<b>
Puts a word in bold. No additional outcome. Example: That's a computer
<strong>
Item in bold and importance put on the contents. Can be nested inside <strong> to show more importance of sections. Example: No, don't do that!
<i>
Used to display different voice or mood and a few other reasons. No extra meaning like <b>. Example: Whose idea was that?
<em>
Used for emphatic stress. Example: I will make it legal
<abbr>
Abbreviation and acronyms Example: LOL
<address>
Email address or postal address of an author/owner of content. Not billinging address etc. Example:
christiankennethbrown@gmail.com
<q>
Used for quotes Example: They told me he fixed it!
<blockquotes>
Used for block quotes Example:
Landos not a system. He's a man. Lando Clrissian. He's a card player, gambler, scoundrel. You'd like him.
<cite>
Name of the work the quote came from. Example: Han Solo <cite>The Empire Strikes Back</cite>
<pre>
Keep whitespace Example: 
 There is still a chance to save       Haaaaaaan

Often used with <code>
